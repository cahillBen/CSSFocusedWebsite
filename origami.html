Origami

Origami (折り紙) is the japanese art of folding paper. I started learning at 11 years old so there's something for any age group. It can be challenging to learn new folds but once you've had a bit of practice it becomes a very relaxing activity.

Making a Crane

One of the most famous designs in all of origami. Beginners will find this to be a difficult but rewarding challenge.

Step 1:

Take your square paper, fold diagonally from corner to corner on both sides.

Fold in half vertically and horizontally.

Bring the right and left corners down to the bottom.

Step 2:

Bring the top corner to make a square base.

Flatten on creases.

Note the two flaps on the right and left corners.

We will work with all four flaps in the next step.

Step 3:

Bring the bottom sides in line with the centre and crease the paper well.

Do this on both sides, back and front.

Fold the top corner down on the lines made by the flaps.

Step 4:

Take the first layer from the bottom and bring it above the top corner, fold along the creases from Step 3.

The fold will make a diamond shape longer in height than it is in width.

Do this on the back and front.

Step 5:

Bring bottom sides into centre and crease.

Do this on Front and Back.

Make sure the bottom corners are formed evenly.

These will form the head and tail, they can either make or break the aesthetic.

Step 6:

Bring the bottom sides in line with the centre and crease the paper well.

Do this on both sides.

Back and front.

Step 7:

Fold the legs up the centre between the wings.

Make the same fold again to form the head.

Fold down the wings.
